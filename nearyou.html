San Fransisco Zoo Monkeys

Nearly 15 primate species were housed in the PDC (Primate Discovery Center), allowing guests to view some of the most endangered primate species in the world, including François’ langurs, black howler monkeys, siamangs, colobus monkeys, squirrel monkeys, lemur species, and pied tamarins.

Carlos the Banana Thief

Carlos has been roaming the streets of California for decades, ravaging local supplies of Bananas. He executes quick hit and run operations local shops and homes to retrieve his bananas. Police have offered 1,000 dollars in exchange for any information on Carlos's whereabouts.

The wild monkeys of thousand oaks

Monkeys were first reported in Thousand Oaks, CA, a suburban city on the eastern edge of Ventura County and about 40 miles northwest of Los Angeles, by residents to local animal control and police in 1995. The reports usually cited very brief sightings and included a description of some mischief such as fruit being stolen off trees, laundry being stolen from close lines, and on rare occasions use of pools and spas.
